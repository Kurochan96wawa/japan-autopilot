Where to Buy Diapers and Baby Formula in Japan

Traveling to Japan with little ones often comes with a unique set of logistical considerations, and ensuring you have access to essential items like diapers and baby formula is certainly top of mind for many parents. The good news is that Japan is exceptionally well-equipped with amenities for families, and these items are widely available. This guide will help you understand where to find them, popular Japanese brands, and how sizing compares.

Key Places to Purchase Diapers and Formula

  • Drugstores (ドラッグストア - Drug Sutoa): These are your primary go-to for baby essentials. Major chains like Matsumoto Kiyoshi (マツモトキヨシ), Sundrug (サンドラッグ), Welcia (ウエルシア), and Cocokara Fine (ココカラファイン) are ubiquitous. They offer a wide selection of brands and sizes, often at competitive prices. Look for dedicated baby sections.
  • Supermarkets (スーパーマーケット - Sūpāmāketto): Larger supermarkets such as Aeon (イオン), Ito-Yokado (イトーヨーカドー), and Seiyu (西友) will stock a good range of both diapers and formula. Their baby aisles are usually clearly marked.
  • Department Store Baby Sections (デパートのベビー用品売り場 - Depāto no Bebī Yōhin Uriba): High-end department stores like Takashimaya (高島屋) or Isetan (伊勢丹) often have extensive baby and children's departments. While potentially pricier, they can offer a wider array of specialized items or imported brands, and the shopping environment is typically very pleasant.
  • Convenience Stores (コンビニ - Konbini): Chains like 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson are found on almost every street corner. They typically carry a limited selection of diapers (often smaller packs or individual pull-ups) and single-serve or small cans of formula, perfect for emergencies or unexpected needs. Prices here will generally be higher than drugstores or supermarkets.

Understanding Diapers (おむつ - Omutsu) in Japan

Japanese diapers are renowned for their quality, softness, and absorbency. You'll find many parents worldwide praise them.

Popular Japanese Diaper Brands:

  • Merries (メリーズ): Known for their exceptional softness and breathability.
  • Moony (ムーニー): Highly regarded for comfort and stretch, often featuring cute designs.
  • Goo.N (グーン): Offers good absorbency and a comfortable fit, often considered good value.
  • Pampers (パンパース): While an international brand, Pampers are also widely available in Japan with formulations tailored to the local market.

Diaper Sizes:

Japanese diaper sizing is primarily based on weight. It's important to note that Japanese baby sizes can sometimes run slightly smaller compared to Western brands for the same weight category, often due to differences in average baby builds. Always check the weight range on the packaging carefully.

  • NB (Newborn): Typically up to 5 kg
  • S (Small): 4-8 kg
  • M (Medium): 6-11 kg (tape), 7-10 kg (pants)
  • L (Large): 9-14 kg (tape), 9-14 kg (pants)
  • Big/XL: 12-22 kg

Diapers come in two main styles: tape (テープ) for younger babies and pant/pull-up (パンツ) for active toddlers. Pant diapers are very popular for ease of changing. If in doubt, purchasing a size up can be a good strategy.

Understanding Baby Formula (粉ミルク - Kona Miruku) in Japan

Japanese baby formulas meet strict safety and nutritional standards.

Popular Japanese Formula Brands:

  • Meiji (明治): Popular types include "Hohoemi" (ほほえみ) for newborns to 9 months and "Step" (ステップ) for 9 months and up.
  • Glico (グリコ): Their "Icreo" (アイクレオ) line is well-regarded.
  • Morinaga (森永): Offers "Hagukumi" (はぐくみ) for initial stages and "Chirumiru" (チルミル) for older infants.
  • Wakodo (和光堂): Known for "Haihai" (はいはい) and "Gungun" (ぐんぐん).

Formula Types and Sizes:

Formula is predominantly available in powder form, typically in cans. For travel convenience, many brands offer individual stick packs (スティックタイプ - Sutikku Taipu) or small cubes, which are excellent for on-the-go feeding without measuring. While less common, some stores might carry ready-to-feed liquid formula in small bottles, particularly in larger urban centers or department stores.

Important consideration: The specific nutritional composition of formulas can vary by country. If your child has dietary sensitivities or specific needs, it is advisable to bring a small emergency supply of your usual brand from home to last the first few days while you explore options or if you prefer to use your familiar brand throughout the trip. Always carefully check the ingredients list if your child has allergies.

Practical Tips for Parents

  • Translation Apps: Utilize a translation app with a camera function to help read packaging labels, especially for ingredients and sizing details.
  • Don't Overpack: While it's wise to bring a small initial supply, there's no need to pack a full trip's worth of diapers or formula. Japan's excellent retail infrastructure means you can easily replenish your stock.
  • Ask for Help: Store staff are generally very helpful. If you have difficulty finding an item, try showing them a picture or writing the Japanese name (e.g., おむつ for diapers, 粉ミルク for formula).

With a little preparation, sourcing diapers and baby formula in Japan is straightforward, allowing you to focus on enjoying your family adventure.
